About breathtaker

Our Story

breathtaker1 was born from a simple frustration: wind instrumentalists had no intuitive way to control synthesizers. Traditional MIDI controllers are designed with pianists in mind, leaving wind players to adapt their technique to an unfamiliar interface.

As a musician and developer, I spent years crafting a solution that allows saxophone players to use their existing fingering knowledge to control digital instruments naturally, without requiring special hardware.

What began as a passion project has evolved into a MIDI solution that brings the familiar fingering patterns of wind instruments into the digital realm, making electronic music production more accessible for saxophone, flute, and clarinet players.

The Technology

breathtaker1 uses sophisticated key-mapping algorithms to convert keyboard combinations into MIDI notes. Our patented fingering detection system recognizes complex combinations and transitions, allowing for realistic wind instrument emulation.

The software features adjustable latency settings (20-100ms) to match your playing style, along with special note hold modes that help sustain notes when needed. It also includes built-in transposition options for Eb saxophones and Bb tenor instruments.

Currently available exclusively for macOS, breathtaker1 leverages the platform's robust MIDI capabilities and integrates seamlessly with DAWs through the IAC Driver for virtual MIDI routing.

The Creator

Brian Bibb

Creator & Developer

Professional musician and software developer with a passion for music technology. Brian created breathtaker1 to solve a problem he experienced firsthand: the need for wind players to have intuitive control over digital instruments.

By combining expertise in saxophone performance with software development, breathtaker1 was born - a tool designed to understand the specific key combinations and fingering techniques used by wind instrument players.

Project Vision

"The goal of breathtaker1 is to make electronic music production accessible to wind instrumentalists without requiring them to learn a completely new instrument. We want to leverage the years of muscle memory and technique that musicians have already developed."

— Brian Bibb, Creator
